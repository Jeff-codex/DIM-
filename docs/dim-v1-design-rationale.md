# DIM v1 Design Rationale

Date: 2026-03-13

## Goal

The first public draft of DIM should feel like the beginning of a serious editorial brand, not a tech blog, SaaS landing page, or breaking-news portal.

## Chosen direction

- light editorial interface
- typography-first hierarchy
- quiet grayscale structure
- `#D72013` used only as a strong accent
- long-form reading widths and generous spacing

## Why this works for DIM

- The homepage leads with the brand statement before article consumption, reinforcing the editorial identity.
- Article listing behaves like a calm archive rather than a high-velocity feed.
- Article pages keep a single reading column and avoid sidebars or dashboard patterns.
- When imagery is absent, the page still holds together through typography and spacing.

## What was intentionally avoided

- dashboard-style card grids
- aggressive gradients
- oversized shadows
- playful or startup-marketing typography
- heavy animation and storytelling gimmicks

## Phase 2 possibilities

- richer article imagery and illustration system
- editorial issue pages or topic collections
- D1-backed content operations
- lightweight internal publishing workflow
